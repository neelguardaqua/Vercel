FAQs

Common Questions Answered

A Sewage Treatment Plant (STP) is a system that removes contaminants from domestic wastewater and sewage before it is discharged into the environment or reused for non-potable applications like gardening and flushing.
An STP protects the environment by preventing untreated sewage from entering water bodies, conserves freshwater by enabling treated water reuse, and ensures compliance with wastewater management regulations mandated by the CPCB and State Pollution Control Boards.
Treated water from an STP can be reused for gardening, toilet flushing, cooling towers, landscaping, fire-fighting reserves, and other non-potable applications — reducing your dependency on the municipal water supply.
Residential complexes, commercial buildings, healthcare facilities, hotels and resorts, educational institutions, industrial campuses, and government projects all commonly require STP systems as mandated by environmental regulations.
Yes, we offer complete turnkey services including site evaluation, system design, manufacturing, civil coordination, installation, commissioning, operator training, and ongoing AMC-based maintenance support.
Installation timelines vary based on plant capacity and site conditions. Compact residential units typically take 4–8 weeks, while large-capacity commercial or industrial systems may require 10–20 weeks from design approval to commissioning.
